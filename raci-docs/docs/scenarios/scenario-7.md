# Scenario 7 — Designing a Multi-Element Training Event

*Co-design across domains — how both Leads contribute at the intent stage*

---

## Context

> A five-day residential training event is being planned for early-career researchers who need both computational and wet lab genomics skills. The event will contain three informatics elements (variant calling, RNA-seq analysis, data visualisation), two lab elements (DNA extraction, library preparation), and two shared elements (keynote lecture, group data interpretation workshop). This is a new event — design starts from scratch.

---

## Phase 1 — Defining the intent of each element

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A** | Head of LDD | Accountable for commissioning the event and the overall programme commitment. Sets the parameters: audience, duration, budget envelope, partner obligations. |
| **R** | Education Developer(s) | Drives the intent-definition process across all elements — facilitates the co-design conversation with both Leads, synthesises their domain input into draft learning outcomes for each element, and holds the pedagogical coherence of the whole event. |
| **C** | Informatics Training Lead | Co-designs the intent of the three informatics elements. Brings domain expertise to define what learners must be able to do computationally — which tools, which datasets, what level of complexity is appropriate for this audience. This is active co-authorship of the informatics element outcomes, not a review of a draft someone else produced. |
| **C** | Lab Training Lead | Co-designs the intent of the two lab elements. Brings domain expertise to define the practical scope — which techniques, what safety considerations, what learners should be able to do independently by the end. Active co-authorship of the lab element outcomes. |

---

## Phase 2 — Designing the learning structure across elements

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A/R** | Education Developer(s) | Owns the curriculum design — sequences the elements so they build on each other, designs the transitions (e.g. data generated in the lab element feeds the informatics analysis), determines the instructional approach for each element, and writes the full session plan. This is where pedagogical expertise is central: the Lead co-designed the intent of their elements; the Education Developer determines how that intent becomes learning. |
| **C** | Informatics Training Lead | Consulted as the curriculum structure develops — confirms the informatics sequence is scientifically logical and that the proposed approach will work for the audience. |
| **C** | Lab Training Lead | Consulted on the lab sequence — confirms the practical structure is safe, appropriate, and achievable in the time available. |
| **I** | Informatics Technical Developer | Informed of the informatics element designs so environment planning can begin in parallel. |
| **I** | Lab Technical Developer | Informed of the lab element designs so protocol development and equipment planning can begin. |

---

## Phase 3 — Technical build (parallel workstreams)

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A** | Informatics Training Lead | Accountable for informatics environment readiness — validates that the compute environment delivers the learning experience as designed for each informatics element. |
| **R** | Informatics Technical Developer | Builds and configures the compute environment for all three informatics elements. |
| **A** | Lab Training Lead | Accountable for lab element readiness — validates protocols, risk assessments, and equipment readiness for both lab elements. |
| **R** | Lab Technical Developer | Develops and tests all lab protocols and equipment setup for both lab elements. |
| **A/R** | Education Developer(s) | Owns the shared elements (lecture, workshop) and the overall event materials — session guides, participant handbook, and joining instructions. |

---

## What this scenario illustrates

!!! success "Key insight"
    This scenario shows the Lead as **active co-designer of intent** for their elements, not a late-stage reviewer. Both Leads are in the room at the start — each shaping the 'what' of their domain. The Education Developer then takes that input and designs the 'how': the sequence, the transitions, the instructional approach.

    On a multi-element event, the Education Developer's coordinating role is especially critical — they are the only person holding the whole picture. Neither Lead can see across both domains; the Education Developer must.
