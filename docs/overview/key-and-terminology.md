# RACI Key & Terminology

## The RACI letters

| Letter | Meaning | Description |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **R** | Responsible | Does the work |
| **A** | Accountable | Owns the outcome — one person only |
| **C** | Consulted | Input sought before decisions or actions |
| **I** | Informed | Kept updated after decisions or actions |
| **N/A** | Not involved | Has no role in this activity |

!!! tip "A vs R"
    A person can hold both A and R (shown as **A/R**), meaning they both own the outcome and do the work themselves. When A and R are split, the R does the work but the A signs it off. There can only ever be **one** Accountable per activity.

---

## Terminology used in this document

The examples throughout this matrix refer to three levels of training:

### Training element

A single, self-contained component of training — for example, a bioinformatics practical, a wet lab session, a lecture, a workshop, or an online module. An element is the building block.

### Training event

A scheduled training activity, which may contain one element or several. A week-long course at a partner site might contain informatics elements, lab elements, and other activities. A standalone webinar might be a single element. The event is the unit of delivery.

### Programme

A structured sequence of training events across a period of time, for a defined audience or learning goal.

## Why the distinction matters

!!! info "Domain-specific roles in multi-element events"
    The Informatics Training Lead's co-design role applies specifically to **informatics elements**. The Lab Training Lead's role applies to **lab elements**. On a multi-element event, both Leads are active at the design stage — each on their own element.

    The Education Developer coordinates across all elements and holds the overall design.
