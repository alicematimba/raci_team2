# About This RACI

## Purpose

This document sets out how responsibilities are distributed across the Learning Design & Delivery team for activities that require the whole team to work together. It is not a job description — it is a coordination map.

## Scope

The matrix covers the shared education development pipeline: activities that require cross-team involvement. It deliberately excludes work that belongs to individual roles alone.

!!! note "Organisational scope of this document"
    This RACI covers how the LDD team coordinates on shared pipeline work. The Head of LDD operates within a broader organisational structure: the Associate Director has oversight of the wider programme, which encompasses training, conferences, events and related activities. Where decisions carry implications beyond this pipeline — major institutional commitments, strategic programme direction, or cross-programme resource decisions — normal line management and institutional governance applies. This document does not describe the full hierarchy; it describes how this team coordinates internally.

!!! note "What's not in here — and why"
    Every role has responsibilities that fall entirely within their own domain. These do not appear in this RACI because they do not involve cross-team coordination. This is by design, not an omission.

    - **Training Leads** carry significant work outside this pipeline: managing relationships with stakeholders, advising on domain strategy, contributing to learning and training programme governance, and developing the technical and scientific knowledge base to support effective training design.
    - **Education Developers, Technical Developers, and the Digital Learning Developer** similarly have areas of individual professional work — curriculum research, specialist system management, technical architecture decisions — that belong to them alone.

## How to use this document

Job descriptions cover the full scope of each role. This matrix covers the shared part. The two are complementary: neither is a complete picture on its own.

Use this matrix to:

- Understand who to involve at each stage of a project
- Clarify where accountability sits for a specific activity
- Resolve ambiguity about who does what when roles overlap
- Onboard new team members to the pipeline

## Roles in this matrix

| Role | Domain scope in this RACI |
|------|--------------------------|
| Head of LDD | Owns the LDD team's portfolio and strategy within this pipeline. Makes decisions on what runs, what gets resourced, and what gets retired across the team's educational development work. Accountable for commitments made on behalf of the LDD team to faculty sponsors and stakeholders; for commitments with significant strategic, cross-programme or institutional implications, the Associate Director is involved or kept sighted. Acts as the escalation point within this pipeline when issues cannot be resolved at team level — matters that go beyond the pipeline's scope follow normal line management. Not involved in day-to-day pipeline operations unless a decision has strategic or reputational implications for the team. |
| Informatics Training Lead | Computational & data elements — covers tools, data pipelines, analytical approaches, software and computational methods, including bioinformatics. Acts as domain curator and quality gate: ensures approaches being taught are current, meaningful and appropriate for the learner level; flags where methods have been superseded by newer tools or workflows; and confirms that any retained older approach has a clear educational justification. Oversight applies whether content is delivered in-person, virtually or as an online resource. Not required to be the deepest expert in every specific tool, but accountable for judging whether the approach is right for the audience and current in the field. |
| Lab Training Lead | Lab elements — covers experimental approaches, physical and virtual practical activities, safety, equipment, reagents and lab infrastructure. Acts as domain curator and quality gate: ensures methods and protocols reflect current laboratory practice; flags where techniques are no longer standard without educational justification; and confirms all safety requirements are met. Oversight applies whether content is delivered in-person, as a virtual simulation or as an online resource. Not required to be the deepest expert in every technique, but accountable for judging whether the approach is appropriate, current and safe for the learner audience. |
| Education Developer(s) | Owns the design pipeline end-to-end — writes learning outcomes, designs curriculum structure, decides on the assessment approach (which may be formal graded assessment, formative activities, project-based, or no summative assessment depending on the programme), issues design briefs to Technical Developers, coordinates delivery, and manages the overall learning design. Coordinates SMEs who typically write and deliver content; the ED's function is to structure it educationally and ensure it maps to the learning design, not to produce subject matter content. There may be more than one ED working on a programme; this RACI covers the ED function regardless of how many individuals hold it. |
| Informatics Technical Developer | Builds, configures and maintains the computational environments and infrastructure needed for computational & data training — including systems such as JupyterHub, Galaxy, compute clusters and datasets. Advises on technical feasibility during design (what is realistically buildable and maintainable). Works within the design brief issued by the Education Developer; does not make learning design decisions. |
| Lab Technical & Operations Developer | Develops and manages the practical infrastructure for lab-based training — lab protocols, equipment, reagents, consumables, safety setup and logistics. Advises on operational feasibility during design (what is safely and practically deliverable in the lab). Works within the design brief issued by the Education Developer; does not make learning design decisions. |
| Digital Learning Developer | Specialist in instructional design for digital and online content — ensures digital learning experiences are pedagogically effective, not just technically functional. Responsible for online platforms, multimedia production and LMS configuration. Advises on what is feasible and effective in a digital format, similar to how Technical Developers advise on infrastructure feasibility. Accountable for the instructional design quality of digital and online outputs — this is their specialist domain, distinct from the Education Developer's role. Scope is primarily digital and online content, with some involvement in virtual delivery. |

!!! info "A note on the Training Portfolio Lead descriptor"
    The two Training Leads — Informatics and Lab — collectively fulfil what is sometimes referred to as the **Training Portfolio Lead** function: strategic ownership of their domain's training offer, stakeholder engagement at function and approach level, and quality sign-off across the pipeline. This descriptor reflects the breadth of that role beyond the development pipeline itself. Job titles remain as per role profiles; "Training Portfolio Lead" is used informally to describe the scope.

!!! info "Education Developer vs Digital Learning Developer — what's the difference?"
    These roles are complementary but distinct. The **Education Developer** designs the *learning* — learning outcomes, curriculum structure, assessment approach, how content maps to what learners need to know and do. This applies across all delivery formats.

    The **Digital Learning Developer** designs the *digital experience* — when that learning goes online, they ensure it actually works as digital content: appropriate chunking, interactivity, navigation, multimedia treatment, accessibility, and platform capabilities and constraints. Content can be educationally sound but poorly designed for online delivery; that gap is the DLD's domain.

    In practice: the ED defines what the course needs to achieve and how it is structured; the DLD shapes how that translates into an effective online or digital learning experience. On courses that are not digital or online, the DLD may not be involved.

!!! info "A note on Training Lead scope"
    In this RACI, the Informatics Training Lead's oversight covers **computational & data elements** — any training content involving computational tools, data analysis, software or data-driven approaches, regardless of the specific discipline. The Lab Training Lead's oversight covers **lab elements** — any content involving experimental or practical laboratory methods, whether physical or virtual. Neither Lead needs to be the deepest expert in every topic, but both are accountable for ensuring that what is being taught reflects current, meaningful practice in the field — and that where it does not, there is a clear educational reason for it.
