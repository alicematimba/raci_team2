# About This RACI

## Purpose

This document sets out how responsibilities are distributed across the Learning Design & Delivery team for activities that require the whole team to work together. It is not a job description — it is a coordination map.

## Scope

The matrix covers the shared education development pipeline: activities that require cross-team involvement. It deliberately excludes work that belongs to individual roles alone.

!!! note "What's not in here — and why"
    Every role has responsibilities that fall entirely within their own domain. These do not appear in this RACI because they do not involve cross-team coordination. This is by design, not an omission.

    - **Training Leads** carry significant work outside this pipeline: managing relationships with stakeholders, advising on domain strategy, contributing to learning and training programme governance, and developing the technical and scientific knowledge base to support effective training design.
    - **Education Developers, Technical Developers, and the Digital Learning Developer** similarly have areas of individual professional work — curriculum research, specialist system management, technical architecture decisions — that belong to them alone.

## How to use this document

Job descriptions cover the full scope of each role. This matrix covers the shared part. The two are complementary: neither is a complete picture on its own.

Use this matrix to:

- Understand who to involve at each stage of a project
- Clarify where accountability sits for a specific activity
- Resolve ambiguity about who does what when roles overlap
- Onboard new team members to the pipeline

## Roles in this matrix

| Role | Domain scope in this RACI |
|------|--------------------------|
| Head of LDD | Owns the team's portfolio and strategy. Makes final decisions on what runs, what gets resourced, and what gets retired. The accountable person for team-level commitments to faculty sponsors and institutional stakeholders. Final escalation point when issues cannot be resolved within the team. Not involved in day-to-day pipeline operations unless a decision has strategic or reputational implications. |
| Informatics Training Lead | Domain expert for computational & data elements — covers tools, data pipelines, analytical approaches, software and computational methods, including bioinformatics. Holds the quality gate for domain accuracy and appropriateness across all content in this domain. Oversight applies whether content is delivered in-person, virtually or as an online resource. Not required to be the deepest expert in every specific tool or method, but accountable for judging whether the approach is appropriate for the learner audience and learning goals. |
| Lab Training Lead | Domain expert for lab elements — covers experimental approaches, physical and virtual practical activities, safety, equipment, reagents and lab infrastructure. Holds the quality gate for scientific accuracy, safety and practical validity across all lab content. Oversight applies whether content is delivered in-person, as a virtual simulation or as an online resource. Not required to be the deepest expert in every technique, but accountable for judging whether the approach is appropriate and safe for the learner audience. |
| Education Developer(s) | Owns the design pipeline end-to-end — writes learning outcomes, designs curriculum structure, decides on assessment, issues design briefs to Technical Developers, coordinates delivery, and manages the overall learning design. Coordinates SMEs who typically write and deliver content; the ED's function is to structure it educationally and ensure it maps to the learning design, not to produce subject matter content. There may be more than one ED working on a programme; this RACI covers the ED function regardless of how many individuals hold it. |
| Informatics Technical Developer | Builds, configures and maintains the computational environments and infrastructure needed for computational & data training — including systems such as JupyterHub, Galaxy, compute clusters and datasets. Advises on technical feasibility during design (what is realistically buildable and maintainable). Works within the design brief issued by the Education Developer; does not make learning design decisions. |
| Lab Technical & Operations Developer | Develops and manages the practical infrastructure for lab-based training — lab protocols, equipment, reagents, consumables, safety setup and logistics. Advises on operational feasibility during design (what is safely and practically deliverable in the lab). Works within the design brief issued by the Education Developer; does not make learning design decisions. |
| Digital Learning Developer | Specialist in instructional design for digital and online content — ensures digital learning experiences are pedagogically effective, not just technically functional. Responsible for online platforms, multimedia production and LMS configuration. Advises on what is feasible and effective in a digital format, similar to how Technical Developers advise on infrastructure feasibility. Accountable for the instructional design quality of digital and online outputs — this is their specialist domain, distinct from the Education Developer's role. Scope is primarily digital and online content, with some involvement in virtual delivery. |

!!! info "Education Developer vs Digital Learning Developer — what's the difference?"
    These roles are complementary but distinct. The **Education Developer** designs the *learning* — learning outcomes, curriculum structure, assessment approach, how content maps to what learners need to know and do. This applies across all delivery formats.

    The **Digital Learning Developer** designs the *digital experience* — when that learning goes online, they ensure it actually works as digital content: appropriate chunking, interactivity, navigation, multimedia treatment, accessibility, and platform capabilities and constraints. Content can be educationally sound but poorly designed for online delivery; that gap is the DLD's domain.

    In practice: the ED defines what the course needs to achieve and how it is structured; the DLD shapes how that translates into an effective online or digital learning experience. On courses that are not digital or online, the DLD may not be involved.

!!! info "A note on Training Lead scope"
    In this RACI, the Informatics Training Lead's oversight covers **computational & data elements** — meaning any training content involving computational tools, data analysis, software or data-driven approaches, regardless of the specific discipline (bioinformatics, genomics, data science, etc.). The Lab Training Lead's oversight covers **lab elements** — any content involving experimental or practical laboratory methods, whether physical or virtual. Neither Lead needs to be the deepest expert in every topic, but both are expected to judge whether the approach, tool choice, or method is appropriate for the learner audience and the learning goals.
