# Stage 1 — Learning Strategy & Portfolio Planning

This stage covers decisions about what training runs, what gets retired, and how the team responds to new institutional priorities.

---

## Setting annual course priorities

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A/R** | Head of LDD | Owns the portfolio plan and makes final calls on what runs, what doesn't, and where resource goes. |
| **C** | Informatics Training Lead | Consulted on computational & data domain needs, capacity and feasibility. |
| **C** | Lab Training Lead | Consulted on lab domain needs and scheduling constraints. |
| **I** | Education Developer(s) | Informed of the agreed plan so they can schedule design work. |
| **I** | Technical Developers | Informed so they can anticipate infrastructure and build demands. |

---

## Deciding to retire a training event from the portfolio

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A** | Head of LDD | Accountable for the decision — it has resource, stakeholder and reputational implications. |
| **R** | Informatics Training Lead (for computational & data elements) / Lab Training Lead (for lab elements) | Responsible for making the case: reviewing uptake, learner feedback and domain relevance. This is a portfolio-level judgement that sits with the Lead, not a design task. |
| **C** | Education Developer(s) | Consulted — they know the design history and whether content could be repurposed. |
| **I** | Technical Developers | Informed so they can decommission associated environments or platform content. |

---

## Responding to a new institutional research priority requiring new skills

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A** | Head of LDD | Accountable for committing the team's capacity and setting the response strategy. |
| **C** | Both Training Leads | Consulted on what a credible training response would involve in their domain. |
| **R** | Education Developer(s) | Responsible for scoping and designing the new learning offer once the decision is made. |
| **I** | Technical Developers | Informed early so technical build time can be factored in. |
