# Stage 7 — Delivery

This stage covers running live in-person events, managing online programmes, and handling incidents during delivery.

!!! info "Who delivers content?"
    In most cases, **SMEs deliver the content** — whether in-person, live-virtual or asynchronous online. The Education Developer coordinates delivery, ensures sessions run as designed, and provides oversight. They may support or contribute to sessions but are not typically the primary content deliverer. The relevant Training Lead holds accountability for the learner experience within their domain, regardless of format.

---

## Running a live in-person training event

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A** | Informatics Training Lead (for computational & data elements) / Lab Training Lead (for lab elements) | Accountable for the learner experience on the day within their domain. May contribute where specialist oversight or real-time quality assurance requires their input — but A sits here regardless. |
| **R** | SME(s) | Responsible for delivering the domain content — presenting, demonstrating, and guiding participants through the material. |
| **R** | Education Developer(s) | Responsible for coordinating delivery — briefing SMEs, ensuring sessions run as designed, managing logistics, and providing oversight. Liaises with the relevant Training Lead to ensure scientific and technical accuracy is maintained on the day. May support or contribute to sessions but does not typically deliver subject matter content. |
| **R** | Informatics Technical Developer (for computational & data elements) / Lab Technical & Operations Developer (for lab elements) | Responsible for technical support during the session. |
| **I** | Head of LDD | Informed; present only if the event has particular institutional significance. |

---

## Managing a live online training event or asynchronous digital programme

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A/R** | Digital Learning Developer | Owns the learner experience on the platform — monitors, troubleshoots and responds. |
| **R** | SME(s) | Responsible for delivering or presenting the domain content within the online format. |
| **C** | Education Developer(s) | Consulted if learner engagement or learning issues arise that may require design changes. |
| **I** | Informatics Training Lead (for computational & data elements) / Lab Training Lead (for lab elements) | Informed of learner progress and any domain-specific issues — particularly if learners are struggling with the computational & data or experimental content. |
| **N/A** | Informatics Training Lead | N/A on platform operation and management — not in their remit. |
| **N/A** | Lab Training Lead | N/A on platform operation and management — not in their remit. |

!!! warning "Clarification: Training Leads and online delivery"
    The Training Leads' I here is domain-specific — they need to know if learners are struggling with the computational & data or experimental content. Platform operation, learner data and digital delivery mechanics are entirely outside both remits.

---

## Handling an incident during delivery — environment fails or practical goes wrong

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **R** | Informatics Technical Developer (for computational & data elements) / Lab Technical & Operations Developer (for lab elements) | Responsible for diagnosing and resolving the technical issue in the moment. |
| **A** | Informatics Training Lead (for computational & data elements) / Lab Training Lead (for lab elements) | Accountable for managing the situation with learners and deciding how to proceed. Acts as the escalation point for incidents within their domain. |
| **I** | Education Developer(s) | Informed — may need to adjust remaining session activities in response. |
| **I** | Head of LDD | Informed after the fact; involved in real time only if the incident is serious or reputationally significant. |
