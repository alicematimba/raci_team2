# Stage 7 — Delivery

This stage covers running live in-person events, managing online programmes, and handling incidents during delivery.

!!! info "Who delivers content?"
    In most cases, **SMEs deliver the content** — whether in-person, live-virtual or asynchronous online. The Education Developer coordinates delivery, ensures sessions run as designed, and provides oversight. They may support or contribute to sessions but are not typically the primary content deliverer. The relevant Training Lead holds accountability for the learner experience within their domain, regardless of format.

---

## Delivering a training event

Roles are consistent across in-person, live-virtual and asynchronous online formats, with some variation depending on format — noted below.

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A** | Informatics Training Lead (for computational & data elements) / Lab Training Lead (for lab elements) | Accountable for the learner experience within their domain, regardless of format. May contribute where specialist oversight or real-time quality assurance is needed, but A sits here regardless. N/A on platform operation for online events — their I is domain-specific. |
| **A/R** | Digital Learning Developer *(online/digital formats only)* | Accountable and responsible for the learner experience on the platform — monitors, troubleshoots and responds. Not involved in in-person delivery. |
| **R** | SME(s) | Responsible for delivering the domain content — presenting, demonstrating and guiding participants through the material, in whatever format the session runs. |
| **R** | Education Developer(s) | Responsible for coordinating delivery — briefing SMEs, ensuring sessions run as designed, managing logistics and providing oversight. For online events, consulted if learner engagement or design issues arise. |
| **R** | Informatics Technical Developer (for computational & data elements) / Lab Technical & Operations Developer (for lab elements) *(in-person and live-virtual)* | Responsible for technical support during the session. Not involved in asynchronous online programmes unless a specific technical issue arises. |
| **I** | Head of LDD | Informed; present only if the event has particular institutional significance. |

!!! warning "Training Leads and online delivery"
    For online and digital programmes, the Training Leads are Informed on domain-specific learner progress — particularly where learners are struggling with computational & data or experimental content. Platform operation, learner data and digital delivery mechanics are not in their remit.

---

## Handling an incident during delivery — environment fails or practical goes wrong

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **R** | Informatics Technical Developer (for computational & data elements) / Lab Technical & Operations Developer (for lab elements) | Responsible for diagnosing and resolving the technical issue in the moment. |
| **A** | Informatics Training Lead (for computational & data elements) / Lab Training Lead (for lab elements) | Accountable for managing the situation with learners and deciding how to proceed. Acts as the escalation point for incidents within their domain. |
| **I** | Education Developer(s) | Informed — may need to adjust remaining session activities in response. |
| **I** | Head of LDD | Informed after the fact; involved in real time only if the incident is serious or reputationally significant. |
