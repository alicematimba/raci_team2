# Stage 4 — Content & Resource Development

This stage covers developing training materials with subject matter experts and producing digital content.

---

## Developing training element materials with a subject matter expert (SME)

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A/R** | Education Developer(s) | Owns and drives the collaboration — shapes what the SME contributes and ensures it serves the learning design. The SME typically writes the content and delivers it; the Education Developer structures it educationally, ensures it maps to the learning outcomes, and does not produce subject matter content themselves. |
| **C** | Informatics Training Lead (for computational & data elements) / Lab Training Lead (for lab elements) | Consulted on approach currency and validity — reviews whether the computational & data or experimental approaches and methods used are current, appropriate for this learner level, and reflect meaningful practice in the field. This applies equally to online and virtual content as to in-person materials. |
| **I** | Head of LDD | Informed; brought in only if the SME relationship is senior or politically sensitive. |
| **N/A** | Technical Developers | Not involved in content development with SMEs. |

---

## Producing a video or multimedia learning resource

*(Where a Digital Learning Developer is involved)*

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A** | Education Developer(s) | Accountable for the learning intent — defines outcomes, provides the content specification, and signs off that the finished resource achieves what it was designed to achieve. |
| **A/R** | Digital Learning Developer | Accountable for the instructional design quality of the digital output — how content is structured, chunked, paced and treated for the digital format. Also responsible for production: filming, editing, animation, interactive build. The DLD is not just executing a brief; they bring specialist expertise in what makes digital content effective and are accountable for that quality. |
| **C** | Informatics Training Lead (for computational & data elements) / Lab Training Lead (for lab elements) | Consulted on domain accuracy — checks that computational & data or experimental content is correct and appropriate for the audience. |
| **I** | Head of LDD | Informed on completion, or if there is a significant resource or scope question. |

!!! info "How ED and DLD accountability works in practice"
    Both the ED and DLD hold A here, but for different things. The ED is accountable for *what* the resource teaches and whether it maps to the learning outcomes. The DLD is accountable for *how* that content is designed and delivered as a digital experience. A resource can fail on either dimension independently — scientifically sound but poorly designed for screen; visually polished but not mapped to outcomes. Both sign-offs are required.

## Adapting existing materials for a new audience or context

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A/R** | Education Developer(s) | Owns the adaptation — determines what needs to change for the new context and makes the changes. |
| **C** | Informatics Training Lead (for computational & data elements) / Lab Training Lead (for lab elements) | Consulted where the adaptation involves substantive changes to computational & data or experimental approaches — including checking that adapted content still reflects current and appropriate methods, and including adaptations for virtual or online formats. |
| **I** | Technical Developers | Informed if any changes affect the technical environment or platform. |
| **I** | Head of LDD | Informed if the adaptation is significant enough to affect the portfolio plan. |
