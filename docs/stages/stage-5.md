# Stage 5 — Technical Build & Infrastructure

This stage covers building computational environments, developing lab protocols, and setting up training on learning management systems.

---

## Building a computational training environment (e.g. JupyterHub, Galaxy, virtual labs)

This covers any technical environment needed to deliver computational & data elements — including hosted platforms, virtual environments, and online learning tools.

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A** | Informatics Training Lead | Accountable for environment readiness — signs off that it is fit for learners and appropriately reflects the computational & data approaches intended. Per the JD: provides 'assurance of environment readiness prior to course delivery.' |
| **A/R** | Informatics Technical Developer | Accountable and responsible for the build, configuration and technical performance. The Informatics Training Lead is not responsible for day-to-day technical implementation. |
| **C** | Education Developer(s) | Consulted — confirms the environment supports the planned exercises and aligns with the learning design. |
| **N/A** | Lab Training Lead | Not involved — different domain. |
| **N/A** | Lab Technical Developer | Not involved — different domain. |
| **N/A** | Digital Learning Developer | Not involved in computational infrastructure unless content is also being hosted on the LMS. |

!!! info "Two people hold A — for different things"
    Two people hold A here for different things, which is valid. The Informatics Training Lead is A on **learner readiness** — does the environment deliver the computational & data learning experience as intended? The Informatics Technical Developer is A/R on the **technical build** — is the infrastructure correctly built and configured? These are distinct and do not conflict.

---

## Developing and validating a lab protocol for a new practical

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A** | Lab Training Lead | Accountable for scientific sign-off and safety — the quality gate. Per the JD: 'own and formally sign off all laboratory risk assessments.' Oversight covers physical practicals and any virtual or simulated equivalents. |
| **A/R** | Lab Technical & Operations Dev | Accountable and responsible for the build and technical integrity of the protocol. The Lab Training Lead is not responsible for day-to-day lab operations. |
| **C** | Education Developer(s) | Consulted on whether the protocol can be delivered within session time and at the right learner level — including where a physical practical is being adapted to a virtual or online format. |
| **N/A** | Informatics Training Lead | Not involved — different domain. |
| **N/A** | Informatics Technical Developer | Not involved — different domain. |

---

## Setting up a training event on the LMS (FutureLearn or internal platform)

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A/R** | Digital Learning Developer | Owns and configures the course on the platform — this is their core domain. |
| **C** | Education Developer(s) | Consulted to ensure the platform structure reflects the intended course flow. |
| **I** | Informatics Training Lead (for computational & data elements) / Lab Training Lead (for lab elements) | Informed when the course shell is ready for their domain content — their involvement is domain content review, not platform management. |
| **N/A** | Informatics Training Lead | N/A on platform setup and configuration — informed only when computational & data content is ready to be loaded. |
| **N/A** | Lab Training Lead | N/A on platform setup and configuration — informed only when lab content is ready to be loaded. |

!!! warning "Clarification: Training Leads and LMS"
    Platform setup, configuration and management are outside both Training Lead remits. Their I is limited to knowing their domain content is going online and confirming it is accurate once loaded — not managing the platform itself.
