# Sign-off Gates & Audit Trail

This appendix lists every formal sign-off point in the pipeline — where work must be approved before proceeding, who holds accountability for that approval, and what they are signing off on.

!!! info "Purpose"
    Sign-off gates create a clear audit trail: evidence that the right person reviewed the right thing at the right stage. They also protect the team by making explicit that approval was sought and given before significant resource was committed.

---

## Sign-off gate summary

| Gate | Pipeline stage | Accountable sign-off | What is being approved | Triggers if missing |
|------|---------------|----------------------|------------------------|---------------------|
| **Scoping sign-off** | End of Stage 2 | Training Lead(s) (for their domain) | The approaches, tools or methods proposed for the course are current, meaningful and appropriate for the audience. Design work should not proceed until this is confirmed. | Learning design is built around outdated or inappropriate approaches; wasted effort if major changes are needed at Stage 6. |
| **Portfolio commitment** | Stage 2 — Agreeing scope with faculty sponsor | Head of LDD | The team has the capacity and capability to deliver what has been agreed with the faculty sponsor. | Unmanageable commitments or scope creep. |
| **Learning outcomes and curriculum sign-off** | Stage 3 | Training Lead(s) (for their domain) | Outcomes are based on current approaches, appropriate for the learner level, and the curriculum structure is domain-credible. | Design brief is issued without confirmation that the content direction is sound. |
| **Design brief issued** | Stage 3 — Design handoff | Education Developer (A/R) | The brief accurately captures learning intent, element requirements and domain scope before technical build begins. | Technical Developers begin work without a clear, agreed specification. |
| **Domain currency, approach validity and quality sign-off** | Stage 6 | Training Lead(s) (for their domain) | Approaches and methods being taught are current, valid and appropriate; any older approaches have documented educational justification. | Content that is outdated or methodologically inappropriate reaches learners. |
| **Learning design quality sign-off** | Stage 6 | Education Developer(s) | Overall instructional design — outcomes, structure, content organisation, and assessment approach where included — is sound and maps correctly to what was designed. | Pedagogically weak or misaligned content is delivered. |
| **Digital instructional design sign-off** *(if applicable)* | Stage 6 | Digital Learning Developer | Digital or online content works as an effective learning experience — appropriately chunked, interactive, accessible and platform-appropriate. | Online content that is technically functional but pedagogically ineffective reaches learners. |
| **Technical readiness sign-off** | Stage 6 | Training Lead(s) (for their domain) | The full learner experience — environment, materials, logistics — is ready to run. | A course runs with unresolved technical, environmental or logistical issues. |

---

## Notes on scoping sign-off

The scoping sign-off at the end of Stage 2 is the most important gate in the pipeline — and the one most easily skipped. Before full learning design begins, the relevant Training Lead(s) should confirm:

- The approaches, tools or methods proposed are current and still used in active research or practice
- They are appropriate for the intended learner level
- If any older approach is included, a clear educational rationale exists (e.g. as a foundation for understanding current methods)
- The scope is deliverable within the team's capacity

This confirmation should be documented — even informally — before the Education Developer begins detailed curriculum design. If it is not sought, the risk is that significant design effort is invested in content that needs to be substantially revised at Stage 6.

## Where sign-off fits in the pipeline

```
Stage 2  →  Scoping sign-off (Training Leads) + Portfolio commitment (Head of LDD)
Stage 3  →  Learning outcomes & curriculum sign-off (Training Leads)
            Design brief issued (Education Developer)
Stage 6  →  Domain currency & approach sign-off (Training Leads)
            Learning design quality sign-off (Education Developer)
            Digital instructional design sign-off (Digital Learning Developer, if applicable)
            Technical readiness sign-off (Training Leads)
```

For the escalation routes that apply when sign-off is contested or delayed, see [Escalation Pathways](escalation-pathways.md).
