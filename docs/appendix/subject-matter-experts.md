# Subject Matter Experts in the Pipeline

Subject matter experts (SMEs) are the scientists, researchers, clinicians, and technical specialists who provide the content knowledge that our courses are built on. They may be Sanger researchers, faculty at partner institutions, or external domain specialists. Their contribution is essential — but it is important to be precise about what that contribution is, and what it is not.

---

## Why SMEs do not appear as columns in the RACI

The RACI matrix covers roles within the Learning Design and Delivery team. SMEs are external contributors — they are not members of the team and do not hold accountability for the educational product. Including them as role columns would misrepresent their relationship to the work.

This does not diminish their importance. It clarifies it. When an SME provides content for a course, the Education Developer is Accountable for how that content is used. When an SME reviews materials for domain accuracy, the Training Lead is still Accountable for the final sign-off. The SME's input informs the team's decisions; it does not replace them.

---

## What SME involvement looks like in RACI terms

| RACI | SME activity | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **C** | Content contribution | An SME is consulted when their domain expertise is needed to shape a piece of content, confirm approach validity, or advise on what learners in their field need to know. Their input is sought before content is finalised. This is always C. |
| **N/A** | Educational design | SMEs do not design learning — they provide the raw material that the Education Developer shapes into a learning experience. An SME who writes a lecture does not own the learning outcomes, the assessment approach, or the instructional structure. Those belong to the team. |
| **N/A** | Accountability | SMEs are never A on any activity in this pipeline. Accountability for the quality, accuracy, and safety of what is taught sits with the team — specifically the Education Developer (A on design and delivery) and the Training Lead (A on domain quality gate). If an SME contributes content that needs updating, the responsibility for catching that lies with the team's review process. |

!!! info "The key principle"
    SMEs tell us what is scientifically true, current, and relevant in their field. The team determines how that knowledge is structured, sequenced, and delivered so that learners can access it effectively. An SME who reviews a script for factual accuracy is acting as Consulted (C). They are not accountable for how that content is taught, assessed, or evaluated — that accountability sits with the Education Developer and, where relevant, the Training Lead.

---

## The boundary between SME and Training Lead

A question that often arises: if a Training Lead reviews content for domain accuracy, what is the SME reviewing it for? The distinction is one of accountability and expertise type:

| Role | Review type | Nature of review |
|------|------------|-----------------|
| **SME** | Practitioner review | Confirms whether the content reflects current practice in their specific domain. Their review is informal and advisory. |
| **Training Lead** | Quality gate review | Confirms whether the content is appropriate for the learner group, meets the team's standards, and is safe and valid to deliver. Their review is a formal accountability gate. |

In practice: an SME might confirm that a particular pipeline is valid science in their lab. The Training Lead confirms it is the right pipeline for this learner group at this level, in this context, and that the approach is still current and meaningful in the wider field. Both inputs are needed — but only the Training Lead's carries formal accountability.

## Worked example — developing a new genomics short course

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A** | Head of LDD | Accountable for commissioning the course and the overall partnership with any external SMEs. |
| **A/R** | Education Developer(s) | Drives the SME relationship — commissions content, briefs SMEs on learning outcomes, reviews what they produce, and shapes it into a learning experience. Accountable for the educational quality of the final course. |
| **C** | Informatics Training Lead | Consulted on whether the content is current, valid and appropriate for the learner group. Holds the quality gate — the SME provides input; the Training Lead signs off. |
| **C** | Subject Matter Expert(s) | Consulted throughout content development — provide domain knowledge, review scripts and exercises for approach validity, and may deliver sessions. They are external contributors whose input the team synthesises; they are not accountable for the course. |
| **N/A** | Lab Training Lead | Not involved unless the course covers wet lab content. |

!!! warning "A common misunderstanding"
    Because SMEs often have greater domain depth than anyone else on the team, there can be an assumption that they should also hold accountability for the course content. They should not — and asking them to would be unfair. SMEs are contributing their expertise, not taking on educational governance responsibility. The team's accountability structures exist precisely so that SMEs can contribute freely without being held responsible for how that contribution is used in a pedagogical context.
