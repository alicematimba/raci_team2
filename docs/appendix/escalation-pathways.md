# Escalation Pathways

Most pipeline activities are handled within the team without escalation. The table below maps the specific situations where an issue should be raised to a different role, who raises it, and where it goes.

!!! info "Principle"
    Escalation is not a failure — it is the correct response when a decision is outside someone's authority or when a situation carries risk beyond the immediate activity. The pipeline is designed so that most escalations resolve quickly at the next level up.

---

| Situation | Who raises it | Escalates to | Notes |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|-------|
| Feasibility concern on design brief | Technical Developer | Education Developer | Must be raised at brief sign-off — not after build has started |
| Domain accuracy issue during content development | Education Developer | Relevant Training Lead | Consult before content is finalised or sent to production |
| SME relationship is senior or politically sensitive | Education Developer | Head of LDD | Inform and agree approach before engaging |
| Scope disagreement on design brief cannot be resolved | Education Developer | Head of LDD | Do not proceed with build until resolved |
| Training Lead raises concerns about computational & data tool or approach | Informatics Training Lead | Education Developer (then SME if needed) | ED assesses impact on learning design and manages resolution |
| Training Lead raises concerns about experimental method or safety | Lab Training Lead | Education Developer (then SME if needed) | ED assesses impact on learning design and manages resolution |
| Incident during delivery — environment fails or practical goes wrong | Technical Developer | Relevant Training Lead | Immediate — Lead manages learners and decides how to proceed |
| Incident is serious, unsafe, or reputationally significant | Training Lead | Head of LDD | Real-time escalation — do not wait until after the event |
| Feedback or review findings suggest portfolio-level change | Training Lead or Education Developer | Head of LDD | Raise at next review cycle, or sooner if urgent |
| External partnership raises strategic, legal or institutional questions | Education Developer | Head of LDD | Do not proceed without Head of LDD involvement |
| Content cannot be made domain-accurate within the learning design constraints | Education Developer | Training Lead + Head of LDD | Three-way conversation to resolve scope or resource |

---

## Where escalations fit in the pipeline

Escalations broadly cluster around three points:

**At the brief stage (Stage 3)** — feasibility concerns, scope disagreements and SME sensitivities surface here. Resolving them early prevents costly rework later.

**During development and QA (Stages 4–6)** — domain accuracy issues, format concerns and technical blockers arise as content is built and reviewed. Training Lead input at this stage prevents problems reaching delivery.

**At delivery (Stage 7)** — incidents and real-time issues require fast escalation to the Training Lead. Only serious or institutional incidents go to the Head of LDD in real time.

!!! note "Escalation beyond this pipeline"
    This table covers escalation within the LDD team's educational development pipeline. Issues that require decisions at programme level — affecting the broader Research Learning & Conferences portfolio, significant institutional commitments, or cross-programme resource decisions — are handled through normal line management. The Head of LDD escalates to the Associate Director as appropriate; that boundary is outside the scope of this document.
