# Scenario 4 — Annual Portfolio Review and Next-Year Planning

*Strategy — who shapes the portfolio and who is consulted*

---

## Context

> At the end of each academic year the team reviews what ran, what worked, what the data says, and what to commission or retire for the following year. This involves evaluation data, stakeholder input, capacity planning, and budget.

---

## RACI for this scenario

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A/R** | Head of LDD | Owns the portfolio review process and the final planning decisions. Presents the plan to Research Learning & Conferences leadership and holds the budget accountability for the LDD team's training portfolio. |
| **C** | Informatics Training Lead | Consulted on informatics provision — what worked, what needs updating, what the scientific community needs next, and capacity constraints for technical environments. |
| **C** | Lab Training Lead | Consulted on the lab programme — practical performance, health and safety incidents, reagent costs, and what the wet lab community is asking for. |
| **C** | Education Developer(s) | Consulted on course-level evaluation findings, learner feedback themes, and design improvements recommended for existing courses. |
| **C** | Digital Learning Developer | Consulted on FutureLearn course performance, platform analytics, and any digital learning opportunities or constraints for the coming year. |
| **I** | Informatics Technical Developer | Informed of decisions that affect the technical roadmap for the next year — e.g. which courses are being retired, which new ones will require environment work. |
| **I** | Lab Technical & Operations Developer | Informed of decisions affecting lab provisioning, equipment investment, or SOP updates required for the next portfolio. |

---

## What this scenario illustrates

!!! success "Key insight"
    'Consulted' is not passive inclusion — each C is a genuine expert input that shapes the Head's decision. But the Head holds A/R: they synthesise all the input and make the call. No individual contributor has A on portfolio strategy.

    The Technical Developers are I because the plan affects them but they do not shape it. This is the correct use of I — they need to know so they can plan, but their expertise is not what determines the portfolio direction.

    This scenario also shows the natural reporting line upward: the Head of LDD synthesises team input and presents to Research Learning & Conferences leadership. Portfolio decisions that have cross-programme implications are handled at that level.
