# Scenario 3 — A Laboratory Practical Fails a Pre-Course Run-Through

*Governance and risk — responding to a safety or quality issue before delivery*

---

## Context

> Three days before a wet lab course is due to run, a full run-through of the DNA extraction practical reveals inconsistent yields and a potential contamination risk. The practical needs to be either fixed or pulled from the programme before participants arrive.

---

## RACI for this scenario

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A** | Lab Training Lead | Accountable for the decision to proceed, modify, or pull the practical. Owns the approach validity and safety sign-off. This is not a decision that can be delegated — the Lab Training Lead carries responsibility for practical safety and fitness for purpose. |
| **R** | Lab Technical & Operations Developer | Diagnoses the problem — identifies whether it is a protocol, reagent, equipment, or environmental issue. Tests proposed fixes and reports back to the Lab Training Lead. |
| **C** | Education Developer(s) | Consulted on the impact of pulling or modifying the practical on the overall course structure and participant experience. If the practical is pulled, they adjust the session plan. |
| **A/R** | Head of LDD | Accountable if the issue requires communication with the host site or partner institution, or if the course itself needs to be rescheduled. The Head decides on any external communication and keeps the Associate Director informed if the situation carries reputational or institutional implications. |
| **N/A** | Informatics Training Lead | No involvement — this is a lab practical issue. |
| **N/A** | Informatics Technical Developer | No involvement — no informatics infrastructure is affected. |

---

## What this scenario illustrates

!!! success "Key insight"
    The Lab Training Lead's accountability is not ceremonial. When something goes wrong in their domain, they own the decision. The Technical Developer does the diagnostic work (R); the Lead decides what happens next (A). The Education Developer is C because the course structure is affected — but they do not make the safety call.

    This scenario also demonstrates why the Head of LDD is I for most day-to-day pipeline activity but becomes A/R the moment an issue has external or institutional implications. The boundary is clear: domain and delivery decisions sit with the team; anything that affects the institution's relationships or reputation escalates to the Head.
