# Scenario 1 — Commissioning and Delivering a New Bioinformatics Course at a Partner Site

*End-to-end lifecycle — from strategic decision to post-course evaluation*

---

## Context

> A partner institution in Nairobi has requested a week-long hands-on bioinformatics course covering variant calling and genome assembly. This is a new course — it does not yet exist in the portfolio. The team needs to commission it, design it, build the compute environment, deliver it on site, and evaluate it.

---

## Phase 1 — Strategic decision and commissioning

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A/R** | Head of LDD | Decides whether to commission the course — weighs strategic fit, partner relationship, capacity, and budget. Signs off the partnership terms and overall programme commitment on behalf of the LDD team. |
| **C** | Informatics Training Lead | Consulted on whether the proposed scope is appropriate and deliverable given current infrastructure and practice in the field. |
| **C** | Education Developer(s) | Consulted on realistic design timeline and whether the learning outcomes are achievable in a week-long format. |
| **I** | Informatics Technical Developer | Informed of the decision and likely timeline so they can plan environment work. |

---

## Phase 2 — Learning design

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A** | Head of LDD | Accountable for the overall quality of the course as a Sanger-branded programme. |
| **R** | Education Developer(s) | Designs the course: learning outcomes, daily structure, session plans, materials, and participant-facing content. Leads engagement with any subject-matter experts. |
| **C** | Informatics Training Lead | Co-designs the intent of the informatics elements — brings domain expertise to shape what learners must be able to do computationally. This is active input at the start of the design process: the Lead defines the 'what' of the informatics element; the Education Developer structures the 'how'. The Lead later provides approach currency and quality sign-off at the QA stage. |
| **I** | Informatics Technical Developer | Informed of the planned content so the compute environment can be configured to match. |

---

## Phase 3 — Technical environment build

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A** | Informatics Training Lead | Accountable for the informatics environment being ready and fit for purpose before delivery. |
| **R** | Informatics Technical Developer | Builds and configures the compute environment — installs software, loads datasets, sets up accounts, tests all pipelines end to end. |
| **C** | Education Developer(s) | Consulted to confirm the environment reflects the learning design — does the setup match what participants will need to do in the sessions? |
| **I** | Head of LDD | Informed when the environment is confirmed ready. |

---

## Phase 4 — Host site logistics and readiness

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A/R** | Head of LDD | Leads the LDD team's engagement with the host institution and is accountable for site readiness overall. For commitments with cross-programme implications, the Associate Director is kept informed. |
| **A/R** | Informatics Training Lead | Leads the informatics readiness assessment — confirms local compute, connectivity, and participant prerequisites meet requirements. |
| **C** | Informatics Technical Developer | Consulted on specific technical requirements for the host site environment. |
| **I** | Education Developer(s) | Informed of any site constraints that may affect the delivery plan. |

---

## Phase 5 — Delivery

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A/R** | Education Developer(s) | Leads course delivery — facilitates sessions, manages participant experience, and is the primary point of contact on the ground. |
| **A/R** | Informatics Technical Developer | Provides live technical support during practicals — troubleshoots environment issues, assists participants, and keeps the infrastructure running. |
| **C** | Informatics Training Lead | Available to support on complex domain questions during delivery if needed. May co-deliver on specialist technical content. |
| **I** | Head of LDD | Informed of progress and any significant issues during the course week. |

---

## Phase 6 — Evaluation and improvement

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A** | Head of LDD | Accountable for acting on evaluation findings and deciding whether and how the course runs again. |
| **R** | Education Developer(s) | Collects and analyses participant feedback. Writes the evaluation report and proposes improvements for the next iteration. |
| **C** | Informatics Training Lead | Consulted on any domain or technical improvements identified from participant feedback. |
| **I** | Informatics Technical Developer | Informed of any environment changes recommended for next time. |

---

## What this scenario illustrates

!!! success "Key insight"
    Accountability shifts across phases — it does not sit in one place throughout. The Head of LDD owns the strategic and quality dimensions; the Education Developer owns design and delivery; the Informatics Training Lead owns domain validity and environment readiness; the Technical Developer owns the build. No single person holds A throughout — it moves to whoever is best placed at each stage.

    This scenario is also a useful illustration of the two-level stakeholder engagement model: the Head of LDD manages the institutional relationship with the partner site; the Informatics Training Lead goes deeper on the technical readiness of the environment at that site. Both are needed.
