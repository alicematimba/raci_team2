# Scenario 6 — A Key Bioinformatics Tool is Deprecated Mid-Cycle

*Reactive technical change — who decides and who acts*

---

## Context

> A widely-used tool in four of the team's informatics practicals announces end-of-life with six months' notice. The team needs to decide whether to switch to an alternative tool, update all affected course materials, rebuild the compute environments, and communicate changes to participants and host sites.

---

## RACI for this scenario

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **A** | Head of LDD | Accountable for the strategic decision — do we switch tools, temporarily suspend affected courses, or find a short-term workaround? This has portfolio and partnership implications. |
| **A/R** | Informatics Training Lead | Owns the domain decision — which alternative tool (if any) is current, appropriate and meaningful for this learner group. Leads the domain assessment and recommends a course of action to the Head of LDD. |
| **R** | Informatics Technical Developer | Tests candidate replacement tools in the compute environment, assesses build complexity, estimates the time required to rebuild, and rebuilds once a decision is made. |
| **R** | Education Developer(s) | Updates course materials — session plans, exercises, participant guides — to reflect the new tool once the domain decision has been made. |
| **C** | Digital Learning Developer | Consulted if any of the affected content has an online or FutureLearn component that also needs updating. |
| **I** | Lab Training Lead | Informed — no lab involvement, but needs to know in case it affects shared scheduling. |
| **I** | Lab Technical & Operations Developer | Informed — no involvement, but informed of any shared infrastructure implications. |

---

## What this scenario illustrates

!!! success "Key insight"
    There are two distinct decisions here and they belong to different people. The Head of LDD decides whether and when to act — weighing portfolio, partnership and capacity implications. The Informatics Training Lead decides what the technically and pedagogically correct action is — which tool best serves the learning in the field right now. Neither makes the other's decision.

    This scenario is a clear example of the Training Lead's curator function in practice: the question is not just "what replaces this tool?" but "what do we actually want learners using, given current practice in the field — and is there a clear educational reason to retain the older approach even temporarily?" The Education Developer and Technical Developer then execute in parallel once the direction is set.
