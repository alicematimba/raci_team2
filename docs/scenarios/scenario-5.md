# Scenario 5 — A New Education Developer Joins and Needs Onboarding to a Course

*Handoff and knowledge transfer — who briefs whom*

---

## Context

> A newly appointed Education Developer is taking over an existing bioinformatics short course from a colleague who is leaving. The course has run three times. The new developer needs to understand the course, the participant profile, the environment, and the relationships.

---

## RACI for this scenario

| RACI | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **R** | Leaving Education Developer | Responsible for handing over all materials, documentation, evaluation history, SME contacts, and course knowledge. Creates a handover briefing. |
| **R** | New Education Developer | Responsible for actively engaging with the handover, asking questions, reviewing materials, and taking ownership of the course by an agreed date. |
| **A** | Head of LDD | Accountable for ensuring the handover happens properly and the course is not left without an owner. Sets the timeline and confirms when ownership has transferred. |
| **C** | Informatics Training Lead | Consulted to brief the new Education Developer on the domain context — the participant community, what the approaches being taught are and why they were chosen, and any domain-specific nuances of the course content. |
| **C** | Informatics Technical Developer | Consulted to walk the new developer through the compute environment — what it is, how it works, and what the Education Developer needs to know to brief participants and respond to environment queries. |
| **I** | Digital Learning Developer | Informed if there is an online component to the course that the new developer will need to interact with. |

---

## What this scenario illustrates

!!! success "Key insight"
    'Consulted' works in a knowledge-transfer context as well as a design context. The Training Lead and Technical Developer are not doing the handover, but their input is essential to the new developer building an accurate picture of the course. Without the Training Lead's C, the new ED has the materials but not the domain understanding. Without the Technical Developer's C, they have the session plan but not a working knowledge of the environment.

    The Head of LDD holds A on the integrity of the transition — not because they do the work, but because a failed handover is a leadership failure. A course that runs poorly because the handover was incomplete reflects on the portfolio.
